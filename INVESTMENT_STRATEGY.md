This is where most beginners either get it wrong (no stops) or get shaken out (stops too tight).

```

### The Tiered Stop-Loss Table:

| Stock Type | Initial Stop-Loss | Trailing Stop (once profitable) | Rationale |
|------------|------------------|-------------------------------|-----------|
| **Blue Chip / Defensive** | -12% to -15% | 10-12% trailing | These recover; give them room |
| **Large-Cap Growth** | -10% to -12% | 8-10% trailing | Moderate volatility band |
| **Mid-Cap** | -7% to -10% | 7-8% trailing | Tighter because moves are sharper |
| **Opportunistic/Tactical** | -7% to -8% | 6-7% trailing | Smallest position + tightest stop |

### Critical Stop-Loss Rules:
1. **Set stops at time of purchase** — not after you're already down
2. **Use closing prices, not intraday** — avoids getting stopped by a wick
3. **Never move a stop-loss DOWN** — only up (trailing stops)
4. **Mental vs. hard stops:** Use hard stops (actual orders) until you have 1+ year experience managing positions emotionally

## 6. 💰 Take-Profit Strategy: Scaling Out

This is the harder discipline. Here's a structured approach:

```

### The 3-Phase Take-Profit System:

| Phase | Trigger | Action | Rationale |
|-------|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Phase 1** | +15% to +20% gain | Sell **25%** of the position | Lock in some profit, reduce risk |
| **Phase 2** | +25% to +35% gain | Sell another **25%** | Now you've recovered your basis in most scenarios |
| **Phase 3** | Beyond +35% | Let remaining **50%** ride with a trailing stop at 8-10% | This is where big winners are made |

### When to take profits FASTER (override the phases):
- Stock has risen 15%+ on **no fundamental catalyst** (just hype)
- Position has grown to exceed your max allocation tier
- Sector is showing signs of overheating (everyone on social media is bullish)
- Earnings are approaching and you're uncomfortable with the risk

### When to be MORE patient:
- Strong earnings beats with raised guidance
- Secular trend still intact (not just a trade, it's a thesis)
- Broad market is in confirmed uptrend

### The Hybrid Rebalancing Model:

| Type | Frequency | What You Do |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Light Check** | Monthly (30 min) | Review positions, check for news, ensure stops are current |
| **Quarterly Rebalance** | Every 3 months | Rebalance allocations back toward targets if they've drifted >3% |
| **Threshold Trigger** | As needed | If ANY position exceeds its max allocation by >2%, trim immediately |
| **Event-Driven** | As needed | Earnings misses, sector shocks, macro changes — reassess affected positions |
| **Annual Full Review** | Yearly | Full strategy review: thesis check on every position, sector allocation audit |

**Why NOT monthly full rebalancing:** Transaction costs, tax drag from short-term capital gains, and over-trading is the #1 portfolio killer for newer investors. Quarterly is the sweet spot.

### Key Technical Indicators to Learn (in order of priority):

1. **200-day Moving Average** — is the stock above or below its long-term trend?
2. **RSI (Relative Strength Index)** — overbought (>70) or oversold (<30)?
3. **Volume** — is price movement confirmed by volume?
4. **Support/Resistance levels** — where has the stock bounced or stalled before?
